=== 1 | chapter 1 ===






 He who is happy with his lot: With the lot that the Holy One, blessed be He, provides him, it is all taken with a good eye. A different understanding - that he has a free spirit and a good heart with that portion that the Holy One, blessed be He, provides him, whether it is good or bad, big or small. And he is not distressed, to pursue and be obsessed about storing and acquiring more than his share. And if he were not joyful from the toil of his hands, what kind of "happy" is there? Behold, even the richest man who worries and is sad about his portion is like the poorest man. Rather [the verse cited] is certainly speaking about one who eats with joy. And since the verse calls him this, hence he is called rich.

=== 2 | chapter 2 ===
"The sages taught in the language of the Mishnah" - That is to say, it is Baraita, and it is repeated in the language of the Mishnah, though it is not Mishnah. And what did they repeat? "Rabbi Meir said" etc. And because until now all the chapters were Mishnah, it was required to specify that from here onwards it is Baraita. And because these are the sayings of legend (Aggadah) and they are told in the occupation of learning Torah, they used to say them in the houses of gathering (Synagogue/Shul) with the rest of these chapters of Tractate Avot:
"Merits many things" - To be righteous and devoted (Hassid) as follows. Worded differently: These many commandments (Mitzvot) are for him:
"Worthwhile for his sake" - That is to say, [the world] was created for him, and so he says (Ecclesiastes 12) "For this is the whole man":
"And [he] is called a friend" - (Psalms 139) "And to me, how precious are Your dear ones, O G-d" [Some translated רעיך in the Psalms passage as thoughts, in this context it is supposed to be dear ones/friends]:
"Beloved" - (Ibid 122) "My those who love you be at peace":
